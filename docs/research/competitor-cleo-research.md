## Overview

Cleo is a UK-founded AI personal finance assistant, launched in 2016 by Barney Hussey-Yeo and incorporated as Cleo AI, Inc. Originally built as a Facebook Messenger chatbot, it has evolved into a standalone iOS and Android app combining conversational AI, budgeting tools, cash advances, savings accounts, and credit-building products.

**Headquarters:** London, UK (incorporated in the US; 99.8% of revenue from the US market)
**CEO & Founder:** Barney Hussey-Yeo
**Founded:** 2016
**Total Users:** 7+ million
**Paying Subscribers:** 1.1 million+ (as of late 2025)
**Total Funding Raised:** ~$175 million across 11 rounds
**Last Known Valuation:** $500 million (Series C, June 2022, led by Sofina Capital)
**Key Investors:** Balderton Capital, LocalGlobe, EQT Ventures, Sofina Capital
**Revenue (2024):** $136 million (run-rate $186M by December 2024)
**ARR (July 2025):** $280 million (growing ~118% YoY)
**ARR (Late 2025):** $300 million+
**Profitability:** Net income positive from August 2024; EBITDA margin of 8.4%
**Geographic Focus:** 99.8% US revenue; planning return to UK and expansion to Canada, Latin America, and Europe

**Tagline/Mission:** "Cleo makes money better." — positioning as the world's first AI financial assistant.

## Product Features & Capabilities

### Core Free Features (All Users)
- **AI Chat Interface:** Conversational AI chatbot (now powered by OpenAI's o3 model) that answers personal finance questions, tracks spending, and provides guidance in natural language.
- **Bank Account Aggregation:** Connects securely to bank accounts via Plaid (read-only), showing all accounts in one unified dashboard.
- **Spending Tracker:** Automatically categorizes transactions, breaks down where money goes, and provides spending summaries by category and time period.
- **Budget Setting:** Users can set and monitor budgets; Cleo tracks progress and sends alerts.
- **Bill Reminders:** Tracks upcoming bills and sends notifications.
- **Basic Financial Insights:** Spending pattern analysis and plain-English summaries.

### Signature Personality Features
- **Roast Mode:** Cleo "roasts" users about their bad spending habits using memes, GIFs, and brutal-but-humorous commentary. When prompted ("roast me"), the AI analyzes recent transactions and delivers pointed, sarcastic feedback designed to shock users into better behavior. This is the app's most viral, differentiating feature.
- **Hype Mode:** The opposite of Roast Mode — Cleo celebrates financial wins with positive reinforcement, encouragement, and praise when users meet goals or improve habits.
- **Personality/Tone:** The chatbot is designed to feel like a "sassy," relatable friend rather than a formal financial advisor. This tone is central to the brand identity and Gen Z appeal.

### Paid Tier Features
- **Cash Advances:** Up to $250 (first-time users: $20–$100). Interest-free; standard transfer (3–4 business days) is free; express same-day transfer costs $3.99–$11.99. Available to eligible subscribers on Plus, Pro, and Builder tiers.
- **High-Yield Savings Account:** 3.33% APY (as of late 2025; previously noted at 2.75% APY effective December 2025 — rate subject to change). Offered in partnership with licensed banking partners (Thread Bank, WebBank).
- **Debt Tracker:** Collects and organizes all debts into a single dashboard, prioritizes payments, and creates a repayment plan on a sustainable timeline.
- **Credit Score Tracking:** Monitors credit score and provides insights on how to improve it.
- **Cleo Builder / Secured Credit Card (Cleo Card):** A secured credit card product designed to help users with no or poor credit history build credit. No credit check required for eligibility.
- **Savings Goals, Hacks & Challenges:** Gamified savings features (e.g., no-spend challenges, round-up savings).
- **Priority Customer Support:** Available on highest-tier plans.

### Cleo 3.0 Features (Launched July 29, 2025)
The most significant product overhaul in the company's history:
- **Agentic Architecture:** Cleo can now autonomously complete multi-step tasks in chat, selecting the right tools and reasoning through complex financial questions without user prompting.
- **Proactive Reasoning (powered by OpenAI o3):** Cleo proactively analyzes transaction history using chain-of-thought reasoning to surface insights before users ask. The company claims Cleo 3.0 achieved an 81% accuracy rate on financial tasks, outperforming leading LLMs from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google on their benchmark.
- **Memory:** Cleo now remembers key details from past conversations (budgeting goals, financial stressors, preferences) to create continuity across sessions.
- **Real-Time Voice Conversations:** Two-way voice interface allowing users to speak with Cleo naturally, including voice-driven financial trivia games.

### Autopilot Feature (Launched February 5, 2026 — US Waitlist)
Cleo's most recent major product launch, described as the next evolution from advice to action:
- **Onramp:** Initial AI analysis of financial situation to establish baseline context.
- **Roadmap:** Creates a long-term plan for a specific financial goal (e.g., emergency fund, debt payoff, major purchase).
- **Daily Plan:** Personalized daily guidance to keep the Roadmap on track.
- **Actions:** Currently recommends actions; roadmap includes automated execution (moving money to savings, preventing overdrafts via cash advance, setting merchant spending limits).
- **Future Autopilot Actions (2026 roadmap):** Bill negotiation, insurance shopping, debt payoff automation, emergency fund building.
- CEO Barney Hussey-Yeo quote: "The challenge with existing financial tools isn't intelligence, it's execution."
- Backed by multi-agent architecture using proprietary tooling and frontier AI models.
- 53% of Americans surveyed say they would trust AI to advise on disposable income; 52% open to AI automatically preventing overdrafts; 48% willing to let AI manage routine bill payments.

## Pricing Tiers

Cleo uses a freemium model with four tiers as of early 2026:

| Plan | Monthly Price | Annual Option | Key Features |
|------|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| **Free** | $0 | N/A | AI budgeting chat, spending tracker, bank aggregation, bill reminders, basic insights |
| **Cleo Plus** | $5.99/mo | ~$44.99/yr | All Free features + cash advances (up to $250), savings goals/hacks/challenges, APY on savings, credit score insights |
| **Cleo Pro** | $8.99/mo | N/A (confirmed) | All Plus features + debt tracking dashboard, AI guidance, additional financial coaching features |
| **Cleo Builder** | $14.99/mo | N/A | All Pro features + Cleo secured credit card (credit building), credit history tools, priority customer support |

**Additional Fees:**
- Express cash advance transfer (same-day): $3.99–$11.99 per advance (in addition to subscription)
- Standard cash advance transfer (3–4 days): Free

**Notes on Pricing:**
- Some sources reference a "Cleo Grow" plan at $2.99/month focused on savings account access with 3.33% APY — this may represent a legacy or regional variant.
- Subscription is required to access cash advances; eligibility for advance amount is determined after subscribing, which was central to the FTC complaint (see Weaknesses).
- Revenue mix (2023 baseline): approximately 59% subscriptions, 41% transaction fees (express transfer fees).
- **CAC:** ~$11 per user; **Payback period:** ~3 months; **Free-to-paid conversion rate:** ~50%.

## Target Market & Positioning

### Primary Target Audience
- **Age:** 18–35-year-olds, with heavy emphasis on Gen Z (born 1997–2012) and younger Millennials.
- **Financial Profile:** People living paycheck to paycheck, those with limited credit history, underbanked or underserved by traditional banks, first-time budgeters.
- **Geography:** Predominantly US market (99.8% of revenue). Originally UK-focused; planning re-entry into the UK and expansion to Canada and Latin America.
- **Psychographic:** Users who avoid or feel anxious about managing finances; people who respond better to humor and informality than traditional financial messaging.

### Brand Positioning
- **"Financial assistant that feels like a friend"** — not a bank, not a rigid budgeting tool. Cleo is positioned as approachable, funny, and relatable.
- **"The world's first AI financial assistant"** — proprietary claim used in all major product announcements.
- Competes most directly with Dave, Brigit, EarnIn, and MoneyLion in the cash advance space; and with YNAB, Monarch Money, Copilot, and Emma in the budgeting/AI assistant space.
- **Non-bank model:** Explicitly not a bank. Partners with Thread Bank and WebBank for licensed financial products. This allows lightweight regulatory footprint vs. neobanks like Monzo, Revolut, or N26.
- **Word-of-mouth:** 60% of new customers arrive through organic word-of-mouth referrals, driven by the viral nature of Roast Mode content shared on social media.
- **Social media presence:** Significant TikTok, Instagram, and Twitter/X presence with viral budgeting content and user-generated Roast Mode screenshots/videos.

### Competitive Differentiation vs. Key Rivals

| Competitor | Key Difference vs. Cleo |
|------------|------------------------|
| **Dave** | Cash advance up to $100 (lower cap); no personality/humor angle; cheaper at $1/month |
| **Brigit** | Up to $250 advance; $9.99/month; more utilitarian, less brand personality |
| **EarnIn** | Up to $1,000 via earned wage access; no subscription required; no budgeting AI |
| **Chime** | Full neobank with SpotMe overdraft (up to $200 fee-free); no AI chatbot or roast feature |
| **Emma (UK)** | Superior bank aggregation; more detailed budgeting; UK-focused; lacks humor/personality |
| **YNAB** | Deep, manual zero-based budgeting; $14.99/month; no AI chat; no cash advances |
| **Copilot** | Best-in-class financial dashboard; $13/month; Apple-only; no cash advances |
| **Monarch Money** | Comprehensive household budgeting; $14.99/month; no AI personality or advances |

## Recent Updates (Last 6 Months — October 2025 to April 2026)

### February 5, 2026 — Autopilot Launch
Cleo's most significant product release of early 2026. Autopilot transitions Cleo from an AI advisor to an AI agent that executes financial tasks autonomously within user-defined guardrails. Initially available to US users via waitlist at meetcleo.com/autopilot. Key capabilities at launch: automated savings transfers, overdraft prevention via cash advance, merchant-level spending limits. Full automation roadmap targets bill negotiation, insurance comparison, and debt repayment management through 2026.

### Late 2025 — $300M ARR Milestone & 1.1M Paying Subscribers
Cleo surpassed $300 million in ARR and crossed 1.1 million paying subscribers, with ARR growing more than 2x year-over-year. CEO Barney Hussey-Yeo publicly posted on LinkedIn asking "London or NYC for the IPO?" signaling the company is actively exploring a public listing. No formal IPO timeline announced as of April 2026.

### July 29, 2025 — Cleo 3.0 Launch
Major platform upgrade. Introduced agentic reasoning (multi-step autonomous task completion), proactive financial insights powered by OpenAI's o3 model, persistent memory across sessions, and real-time two-way voice conversations. Press release claimed Cleo 3.0 achieves 81% accuracy on financial tasks, outperforming models from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google on the company's proprietary benchmark.

### July 2025 — $280M ARR Milestone
Cleo reported $280 million ARR as of July 2025, up from $185 million at end of 2024 — representing approximately 51% growth in 7 months and 118% year-over-year growth.

### March 27, 2025 — FTC Settlement ($17 Million)
The US Federal Trade Commission filed a complaint against Cleo AI, Inc. alleging:
1. Misleading advertising of cash advance amounts (ads promised "hundreds of dollars" but almost no users received the advertised amounts; many received $20–$30 after paying a $14.99 monthly fee).
2. Hiding subscription terms and express transfer fees ($3.99–$9.99) in fine print.
3. Making cancellation deliberately difficult — consumers were blocked from canceling subscriptions while advances were outstanding.

Cleo agreed to a $17 million settlement the same day. The consent order (effective for 10 years) requires:
- Full upfront disclosure of all fees and advance amounts before enrollment.
- Explicit, informed consent before charging subscriptions.
- A simple, easy cancellation process.
- No misleading claims about advance amounts or availability.

This was a significant reputational and regulatory event. The FTC press release was published March 27, 2025.

### Ongoing 2025 — UK Return Plans
CEO confirmed Cleo is planning a return to the UK market (it pivoted entirely to the US in 2018). Expansion plans also include Canada, Latin America, and broader Europe. The company is modeling its international expansion strategy on Revolut's approach.

- **Apple App Store:** 4.6/5 stars — 180,305+ reviews (lifetime average)
- **Google Play:** 4.4/5 stars — 121,000+ reviews (some sources cite 102,573 Google Play reviews)

### Reddit Sentiment
No prominent dedicated Reddit threads appeared in search results, but aggregated review data indicates:
- r/personalfinance users generally skeptical of cash advance apps broadly; Cleo specifically criticized for the subscription-first model where advance eligibility is revealed only after payment.
- UK users largely unaware of Cleo as a current product (Cleo withdrew from UK in 2018 and has not yet relaunched).
- Common Reddit-adjacent theme: users feel the marketing promises large advances but actual eligibility is much lower, reinforcing FTC complaint themes.

### PissedConsumer
261 reviews on Cleo's PissedConsumer profile, with recurring themes of:
- Unauthorized post-cancellation charges
- Advance amount discrepancy vs. advertised
- Questionable credit-reporting entries
- Withheld advances without explanation

### Smart Money People (UK-focused)
Cleo has a presence on Smart Money People, though reviews are limited given the app's US focus. Historical UK reviews praised the chatbot experience and roast mode but noted limitations vs. full-service UK apps like Emma or Monzo.

### Better Business Bureau (BBB)
Active complaints through September–October 2025 continue to flag withheld advances, surprise fees, unauthorized charges, and disputed credit-reporting entries.

### Overall Sentiment Summary
Cleo has a **bifurcated review profile**: strong positive sentiment from users who successfully use the cash advance and budgeting features (lifetime 4.5 stars, high Trustpilot score), but a significant and growing negative segment driven by the subscription/advance mismatch, cancellation difficulties, and the FTC settlement. The November 2025 rating drop to 2.9 (most recent reviews) is a meaningful warning signal, even if the lifetime average remains strong.

## Key Strengths

1. **Unique Brand Personality:** Roast Mode and the sassy chatbot persona are genuinely differentiated in a commoditized space. Drives viral organic marketing and strong word-of-mouth (60% of new users via referral).

2. **Best-in-Class Unit Economics:** CAC of ~$11, 3-month payback period, 50% free-to-paid conversion, and 60%+ gross margins are exceptional for a consumer fintech. This makes Cleo one of the most capital-efficient consumer fintechs at scale.

3. **Strong Financial Trajectory:** $300M+ ARR with 2x YoY growth, profitable since August 2024, crossing 1.1M paying subscribers. IPO optionality in the near term.

4. **AI Product Sophistication:** Cleo 3.0's agentic architecture, memory, voice, and proactive reasoning (OpenAI o3) represent a genuine technical leap. The Autopilot product (February 2026) moves from advisor to executor, a defensible moat if well-executed.

5. **Lightweight Regulatory Footprint:** Non-bank model avoids banking license requirements while partnering with licensed banks for regulated products. Much faster to scale than neobanks.

6. **Gen Z Lock-In:** First-mover advantage in AI-native, personality-driven financial tools targeting the 18–25 demographic. Strong social media presence and culturally resonant branding.

7. **US Market Execution:** One of few European fintechs to successfully crack the US consumer market at scale (7M users, ~$300M ARR). 99.8% US revenue concentration proves genuine product-market fit.

---

## Key Weaknesses

1. **FTC Settlement & Trust Deficit:** The March 2025 $17 million FTC settlement for deceptive practices is a major brand scar. The bait-and-switch dynamic (subscribe for $14.99, discover you only qualify for $30 advance) has generated persistent negative reviews and press coverage.

2. **Subscription-Gated Advance Amounts:** Requiring payment before revealing eligibility is the core complaint across all review platforms. Post-FTC, Cleo must now disclose amounts upfront, but reputational damage is significant.

3. **Cash Advance Caps Are Low vs. Competitors:** Maximum $250 advance (often much less in practice) vs. EarnIn ($1,000), MoneyLion ($500+). For users in genuine financial distress, this can be insufficient.

4. **Declining Recent Ratings:** The drop from a lifetime 4.5 to 2.9 in November 2025 most recent reviews indicates a deteriorating user experience at the margin. App crashes, login failures, and bank disconnection issues are recurring technical complaints.

5. **Customer Support Quality:** Heavy reliance on AI chatbots for support with no easy path to human agents is a consistent, serious complaint. Users report inability to cancel subscriptions, reach representatives, or receive refunds.

6. **Highly US-Concentrated:** 99.8% US revenue means any US regulatory action (like the FTC settlement), economic downturn, or competitive shift has outsized impact. Geographic diversification is still early-stage.

7. **No Investment Tracking / Net Worth Tools:** Unlike competitors like Copilot, Monarch Money, or Personal Capital, Cleo does not provide investment portfolio tracking or net worth monitoring — a gap for users who need holistic financial management.

8. **Cash Advance Express Fees Are Confusing/Hidden:** The $3.99–$11.99 express fee layered on top of subscription fees adds complexity and has drawn complaints of hidden pricing, even post-FTC.

9. **UK Market Absence:** Despite being a UK-founded company, Cleo has no current UK product offering (withdrew in 2018). This leaves the UK market to Emma, Monzo, and Plum — a meaningful gap given Cleo's brand recognition there.

10. **No Cryptocurrency or Emerging Asset Class Support:** No crypto wallet, investment accounts, or brokerage integration — limits appeal for financially sophisticated users.
